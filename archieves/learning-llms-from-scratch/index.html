Learning LLMs From Scratch

This post is a work in progress, where I will document my journey of learning about Large Language Models (LLMs) from scratch. The goal is to build a foundational understanding of LLMs, their architecture, and how they can be implemented.

Initial Steps

  1. Understanding the Basics: Start with the fundamental concepts of machine learning and natural language processing (NLP). Familiarize yourself with key terms and techniques.
  2. Reading Research Papers: Begin with seminal papers in the field of LLMs, such as the Transformer paper by Vaswani et al. (2017) and the BERT paper by Devlin et al. (2018).
  3. Online Courses: Enroll in online courses that cover deep learning and NLP, focusing on modules that discuss LLMs.
  4. Hands-On Practice: Implement simple models using libraries like TensorFlow or PyTorch. Start with basic neural networks and gradually move to more complex architectures.
  5. Building a Simple LLM: Attempt to build a simple LLM from scratch using a small dataset. This could involve tokenization, embedding layers, and attention mechanisms.

Resources

  • Books:
    • “Deep Learning” by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville
    • “Natural Language Processing with Transformers” by Lewis Tunstall, Leandro von Werra, and Thomas Wolf
  • Online Courses:
    • Coursera’s “Natural Language Processing Specialization” by Deeplearning.ai
    • Fast.ai’s “Practical Deep Learning for Coders”
  • YouTube Channels:
    • Yannic Kilcher’s channel for in-depth explanations of research papers
    • Two Minute Papers for quick summaries of recent advancements in AI
  • GitHub Repositories:
    • Hugging Face’s Transformers library for practical implementations of LLMs
    • OpenAI’s GPT-2 and GPT-3 repositories for understanding large-scale models
